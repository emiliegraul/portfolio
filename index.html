My name is Emilie Graul, and I am a Project Manager and Designer working for start-ups in San Francisco. I love making all the pieces of the puzzle fit together, and would love to get started with your team! Below I’ve outlined some of the highlights of the past few years of my career.

Colour Studio

Project Manager & Design Associate

2017 - 2019

Deliverables included thorough diagrams of building details like these unique facades you see around San Francisco.

The Colour Studio is a boutique color consultancy working primarily with architecture firms in the Bay Area. As Project Manager and Design Associate, I was responsible for leading many local and national projects.

I assisted in generating proposals, outlining scope, delegating project tasks, and the daily coordination with clients, architects, facility managers, developers, contractors, designers, etc.

One of the highlights of my job was being able to work on the older, more ornate buildings of San Francisco. We had more freedom with these projects to propose bright and intricate schemes. It’s a pleasure of mine to spot building exteriors around the city that I had a hand in creating.

Color was meticulously matched from screen to print to real-life application, guaranteeing an accurate portrayal from start to finish.

During my time at Colour Studio I drove many projects to completion, including multiple 100+ unit residential projects around San Francisco, Oakland, Mountain View, San Diego, and Los Angeles.

Our projects ranged in size from single unit Vicotrians in residential neighborhoods, to multi-million dollar renovations like that of the USCF campus.

With years of experience serving as a project manager and designer, I have built up experience with Adobe’s Creative Suite, and general management tools like Asana, Excel, Concur, etc.

Hack Reactor

Operations Associate

2015 - 2017

Hack Reactor is a premier coding bootcamp in San Francisco. During a three month program, students are trained as software engineers and guided into the industry. As an Operations Associate, I had a front-facing role with students and coworkers.

Students paired up in our office working on coding challenges. We supported 4 cohorts of 40+ students at a time.

Constant office upkeep was critical as students and staff would be in the space from early morning to late at night. It was important to ensure everything was properly cleaned and stocked every day for the 50+ staff members and 200+ students.

I was always on hand to resolve any IT related issues for students and staff.

During my time at Hack Reactor, as the company grew and acquired smaller bootcamp start-ups, I assisted in the moving and opening of 3 locations.

By the way! I most recently spent 5 months traveling around the world, visiting 12 different countries. I met some amazing people, stayed in some beautiful cities, and ate some delicious food. I learned a lot about different cultures, but also about myself in the process. Ask me about it!
